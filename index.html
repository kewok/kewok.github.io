I am an evolutionary ecologist. My goal is to link theory and data in order to understand how community structure drives trait evolution, and elucidate how evolutionary change, in turn, feeds back to structure multi-species communities. I received my Ph.D. in Biology from the University of California, Los Angeles. Since January of 2015, I have been based at the Yale Institute for Biospheric Studies (YIBS) and the Department of Ecology and Evolutionary Biology at Yale University working with the Post, Turner and Vasseur groups. I also work in close collaboration with the Grether group at UCLA to pioneer the emerging field of agonistic character displacement.
